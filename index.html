Sukritacts' Ethiopia: Zara Yaqob

Description Gameplay History
This mod adds the Ethiopian Civilization, lead by Emperor Zara Yaqob, to the game. This version of Ethiopia seems to be primarily inspired by medieval Ethiopia. As a result, it is a civilization with a heavy focus on religion and a bias towards hills and mountains.
  • The leader screen is partly animated! It is nowhere near the quality of the official leaders, but it is a good step up from static leaders.
  • The unique building and unit have unique graphical designs!
  • Zara Yaqob's ability, which can grant you up to two wildcard slots very easily, is unbalanced.
  • Both the Rock-Hewn Church and the Shotelai-unique unit are accurately represented in the gameplay.
  • Ethiopia's unique ability 'Solomonid Dynasty', which allows them to buy settlers, builders and traders with faith, is an interesting but not completely wrong way of representing the state building that the Solomonids did based on religion.
  • Zara Yaqobs' unique ability, which awards him two extra wildcard slots after building a holy site, feels like it is coming out of nowhere. Zara Yaqob was a leader who greatly strengthened the Ethiopian church and it's relation with other Christians. A bonus to inner unity or relations with people of the same faith would feel much more appropriate and less generic.
  • The Civilopedia-entries are unfortunately copy-pasted from Wikipedia.

JFD's Pius IX of the Papal States

Description Gameplay History
This mod adds the Papal States, lead by pope Pius IX, to the game. The Papal State focusses on establishing a religion as quickly as possible. Pius focusses on diplomacy.
  • The mod adds a balanced leader into the game. No technical issues.
  • The Papal State does not have any features that we haven't seen in civilizations that are already in the base game. Playing as Pius feels very generic.
  • The Swiss Guard feels like an accurate representation. It has to be hired with gold and only unlocks after diplomatic service is researched. In this way, it mimicks the actual origins of the Swiss guard as swiss mercenaries.
  • The actual abilities feel a little bit generic, but the names for Pius' unique ability (Vatican Council) and agenda (Papal Infallibility) accurately represent what he worked on during his pontificate.

Infixo's Real Great People

Description Gameplay History
This mod replaces the generic graphics used to represent great people by portraits of the actual person.
  • The portraits use slightly differing artstyles, which works distracting.
  • The mod does a great job at helping you vizualise what these people looked like. One of the great strengths of the Civilization series is the ability to make the player familiar with people and concepts from all over history. After using this mod, players will have an easier time recognizing famous people in history.

Tomatekh's Historical Religions

Description Gameplay History
This mod adds a large number of religions to the game. It also makes sure that AI civilizations try to establish a religion that is as close to their historical religion as possible.
  • The portraits use slightly differing artstyles, which works distracting.
  • The mod does a great job at helping you vizualise what these people looked like. ITCore's Immersive Dialogue

Description Gameplay History
This mod changes the generic dialogue in the diplomacy screen to custom dialoge tailored to each leader and nation. Say goodbye to the generic 'How dare you!'
  • The increased variation in diplomacy will greatly enhance immersion. No longer will your suspension of disbelief be broken by every other player in the game saying the same three things to you. These lines were not voice acted in the base game, so there are no wasted audio files.
  • None of the things that are said are historically inaccurate. There is however a wasted opportunity here. This would've been a great way to work some more historical details into the game, and perhaps some actual quotes from the leaders. Instead, we get varied, but barely any less generic quotes.

Civitas: City States Expanded

Description Gameplay History
This mod adds 4 new types of city-states and over 40 new city-states to the game.
  • The mod greatly enhances the variety of city states encountered.
  • The city states added to the game are not as interesting as the ones in the basegame. They primarily give static upgrades to yields. The sheer amount of city-states added means that you will encounter more interesting ones, such as the city-states added by the Vikings DLC, far less often.
  • The unique benefits for suzerains gained from the added city states feel incredibly random. It lacks the history-based unique benefits that made the city states in Civilization VI much more interesting than the ones in Civilization V.

Rosetta - Dynamic City Names

Description Gameplay History
This mod makes city names change to reflect their new owner when they are conquered by another nation. Naples would for example become Nápoles if conquered by Spain.
  • Barely any impact on gameplay. No noticable bugs.
  • The game now more properly reflects the kind of cultural conversion that would at times occur when a city was conquered by another nation, such as in the example of Constantinople becoming Istanbul. Greatly enhances immersion.

C15 and SeelingCat's Colonial City Names

Description Gameplay History
This mod changes the way new cities are named. When a new city is settled on the same continent as the capital, the regular city name list will be used. If, however, the new city is established on another continent, it will use a name that was used for one of the civilization's colonies. A new city for the Netherlands would, for example, be named 'New Amsterdam' if it was founded on a new continent.
  • Barely any impact on gameplay. No noticable bugs. Compatible with Rosetta - Dynamic City Names!
  • Besides greatly enhancing immersion for those familiar with the period of colonization, the mod will also help new players get familiar with the colonial empires of some nations.
  • Civilizations that lacked a colonial empire will get names that are not representative for their history.
